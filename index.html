Welcome to Lumin Chess
Swap the pieces: every black should end where the whites started, and every white where the blacks were. Solve it in as few moves as you can.
Pieces always move in pairs. Pick two adjacent neighbors — never just one, never diagonal. The pair slides one cell in any direction together.
↗
Single-finger swipe traces over the pair you want to move. Straight along the pair if it slides along itself, or an L — over the pair, then turn in the slide direction — if it slides across.
✌
Two-finger slide — place a finger on each piece of the pair, then drag both together. Direct control — your fingers are the pair.
How to move
Either way works. We'll try both next.
●
Goal markers in each cell show which color belongs there. They fade as you place pieces correctly.
⏱
Time counts. The clock starts on your first move. On ties, faster runs rank higher on the leaderboard.
